How do you want you and your work to be perceived?

First and foremost, I am a prose writer. I have always been most comfortable creating with words. I have very little experience with visual communication, but I am confident that I can become at least competent with such compositions. I have been told, as a Technical Communication major, that a working knowledge of visual communication design combined with good writing skills is very welcome in many sectors.

What visual elements will work? What will not work?

Please refer to my collage for my favorite visual elements that are likely to find their way into my work. My favorite themes include science fiction and, to a lesser degree, fantasy. I am also a bit of an Anglophile; therefore I am drawn to Victorian gardens and everything associated with high tea. Since I prefer organic (if imaginary) elements, anything geometric would not fit in with my style. I know what you're thinking! Yes, spaceships are absolutely organic elements, in my book. This lies at the heart of why I am such a fan of Joss Whedon's short-lived "Firefly" series. The ship, Serenity, was just as much a living character as any of the humans which occupied her interior.

What kind of content will you have: e.g. code samples, logos, essays, etc.

I could show some examples of work that I have done here at EWU, but to be honest, a Technical Communication major's work is precise and practical, but not very entertaining. I have two essays that I wrote for Intercultural Communications class that may be satisfactory. I may also include an original short story or excerpt.
